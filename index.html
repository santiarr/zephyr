How It Works?

Zephyr Batteries are a high-density fuel cell that use an enzymatic pathway to create a lot of electricity from glucose.

Maltodextrin

We use a 15% solution of maltodextrin. Sugars are appealing fuels for enzymatic fuel cells because they are abundant, renewable, inexpensive in terms of $ GJ 1, non-toxic, safe for storage and distribution, and carbon neutral over the entire life cycle.

Enzymatic Pathways

We can produce nearly 24 electrons per glucose unit of maltodextrin through a synthetic catabolic pathway that comprises 13 enzymes in an air-breathing enzymatic fuel cell.

Storage

Our fuel cells have an energy-storage density of 596 Ah kg−1, which is one order of magnitude higher than that of lithium-ion batteries. Sugar-powered biobatteries could serve as next-generation green power sources, particularly for portable electronics.

Why BioBatteries?

Energy Dense

Our sugar-powered fuel cell that has an energy storage density of 596 amp-hours per kilo — one order of magnitude higher than lithium-ion batteries.

Safe

The only biproducts of the battery are electricity and water making these batteries extremely safe. This makes is perfect for implants or residential areas.

Green

These batteries are biodegradable and carbon neutral. This eliminates one of the biggest inconveniences if current battery technology.
